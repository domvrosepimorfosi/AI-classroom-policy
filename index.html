AI Classroom Policy - Α Guide

A clear policy for safe, ethical, and effective AI use at school.

What it is

What is an AI Classroom Policy?

An AI classroom policy is a class‑level agreement explaining if, when, and how AI tools may be used by students and teachers. It sets acceptable and unacceptable uses, disclosure/citation rules, privacy expectations, and consequences for misuse.

Why it matters

Why Is It Important?

  • Creates clear expectations for AI use.
  • Promotes academic integrity and ethics.
  • Protects student data and privacy.
  • Supports equity & inclusion.
  • Aligns AI with learning goals.
Practical tips

Tips for Creating Your AI Policy

  • Co‑create with students.
  • Be specific: allowed tools/tasks & when AI is off‑limits.
  • Include disclosure & citation examples.
  • Show acceptable vs. unacceptable use.
  • Offer no‑AI alternatives and accessibility options.
  • Review & update after assignments.

Components of an AI Classroom Policy

Academic Integrity

Academic Integrity

  • Clarify allowed help (brainstorm, outline, feedback).
  • Require disclosure/citation of AI assistance.
  • Define prohibited uses (auto‑writing, exam help).
  • Note limits of detection tools and consequences.
Data Privacy

Data Privacy

  • Don’t paste personal/identifying information.
  • Avoid confidential or copyrighted uploads.
  • Prefer school‑approved tools; use anonymous prompts.
  • Clear histories; follow age/consent rules.
Ethical Use & AI Literacy

Ethical Use & AI Literacy

  • Teach how gen‑AI works and where it fails.
  • Verify facts; check for bias.
  • Encourage original thinking and process.
  • Collect drafts, prompts, and reflections.
Equity & Inclusion

Equity & Inclusion

  • Ensure access to devices, tools, and internet.
  • Provide no‑cost alternatives & accommodations.
  • Use inclusive examples and materials.
  • Give in‑class time if home access is limited.
Pedagogical Alignment

Pedagogical Alignment

  • Align AI with learning outcomes.
  • Scaffold thinking—don’t replace it.
  • Specify no‑AI contexts (quizzes/exams/first drafts).
  • Reflect on how AI influenced learning.
Discussion prompts

Key Questions to Ask

  • Which AI tools are allowed for this course/assignment?
  • For which tasks is AI help acceptable or prohibited?
  • How should students disclose and cite AI assistance?
  • What privacy rules apply to prompts/uploads?
  • How will we handle bias, errors, hallucinations?
  • What are the consequences for misuse?
  • Where can students get help or ask questions?
Categories

Useful AI Tool Types

  • Planning & brainstorming (ideas, project maps).
  • Writing support (outlining, feedback, tone).
  • Language support (translation, scaffolds).
  • Accessibility (TTS, STT, reading helpers).
  • Presentation & visuals (slides outlines, alt‑text).
  • Data & coding helpers (cleanup, pseudocode).
